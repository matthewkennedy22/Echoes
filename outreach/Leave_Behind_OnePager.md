# ECHOES — Bring Your History to Life

*A free America250 pilot for [ORGANIZATION NAME]*

---

## What it is

ECHOES turns **your own archives** into an interactive experience. A visitor scans a
**QR code** beside an exhibit (or opens a web link) and can **ask a historical figure
questions in plain language** — and every answer comes **only from your validated
sources**, never the open internet.

No app to download. No accounts. No new staff. No IT project.

---

## Why it's trustworthy (not a gimmick)

- **Source-grounded:** answers are drawn from the documents *you* provide and approve.
- **Honest about uncertainty:** every answer is labeled —
  **Documented · Reasonable inference · Contested · Unknown.**
- **Show-the-evidence button:** visitors can see *why* the figure answered that way.
- **Clearly labeled as an AI simulation** based on historical records — never pretends
  to be a real living person.
- **You stay in control:** you set the topics, approve the sources, and can review usage.

---

## What you get (free pilot)

- **One** historical figure or exhibit from your collection, brought to life
- A **QR code + web link** ready to place beside the exhibit or share online
- Simple **analytics**: how many people engaged, how long, and what they asked most
- A finished experience **you keep** — great for America250, school field trips,
  newsletters, and social media

## What I need from you

- Access to **source material** for one figure (letters, speeches, records, photos, notes)
- Confirmation we may use those materials (public-domain, yours, or permissioned)
- ~30 minutes of feedback once it's built

## Why now: America250

This is the United States' 250th anniversary year. State humanities councils are funding
local oral-history and "legacy" projects right now. This pilot is a fast, visible,
low-effort way to take part — and the finished experience is yours to keep.
