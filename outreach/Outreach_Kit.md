# ECHOES — Partner Outreach Kit

**Goal:** land your first pilot partner (a local historical society, small museum, or
library local-history desk) who gives you archive access to **one hero figure or exhibit**.
You're offering a **free** first build tied to America250. They keep the asset; you get a
reference case study.

**How to use this:** fill every `[BRACKET]`. Send 10 emails this week. Call the 3 most
promising 2–3 days after emailing. Track everyone in the tracker at the bottom.

---

## The offer (memorize this — it's the whole pitch)

> "I'll turn **one** figure or exhibit from your collection into a free, interactive
> 'conversation' that visitors can talk to by scanning a QR code — at no cost to you, as
> an America250 project. You keep the finished experience. I just need access to your
> source material and your feedback."

Why partners say yes: it's **free**, it's **fast**, it makes them look **innovative for
America250**, and it requires **no IT work or new staff**.

## Objection handling (quick answers)

- **"Is this accurate? AI makes things up."** → "Yes — that's the whole point of ECHOES.
  It only answers from *your* validated sources, labels each answer (documented / inferred /
  contested / unknown), and shows the evidence. It's built to teach source-checking."
- **"We have no budget."** → "The pilot is free. I'm building reference examples."
- **"We have no tech staff / website is old."** → "No integration needed. It's a QR code
  and a web link. Nothing to install."
- **"What do you get out of it?"** → "A real example to show other organizations, and your
  feedback to improve it. If you love it, we can talk about more figures later."
- **"What about rights to our materials?"** → "We only use public-domain, your own, or
  permissioned material, and you approve everything before it goes live."
